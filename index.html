Biodiversity

The different kinds of life found on earth is known as biodiversity. This includes all plants, animals , and other living organisms present on the Earth.
The term 'Biodiversity' was coined in the year 1985.

Introduction to Biodiversity


Uses Of Biodiversity are as follows -

  1. Helps In Supplying Oxygen.
  2. Increasing the fertility of Soil which helps in increased crop production
  3. Supplies Water so that we can fulfill the daily requirements.
  4. Helps in production of goods and thus, helping in economic growth of a country.
  5. Supplies our daily needs.

Uses of Biodiversity


In the above section , we saw the uses of biodiversity in our daily life. Due to overconsumption of resources provided by the environment and its wastage , we are very dependent on them and slowly we are destroying our environment. For e.g. - Cutting Down Of Trees, Hunting Animals, etc.Pollution is also a great matter of concern as it pollutes water, air, and land and killing animals , plants and even contribute to high temperature. The people's greed has brought some species to the verge of extinction and this destruction of biodiversity will affect our future a lot. Thus, the protection of the environment has become a necessary responsibility for all of us.

Extinction Of Biodiversity


From The Above Paragraph , we came to know that our biodiversity is in verge of extinction in many respects. So , it is our responsibility to save our environment for a better future.
Some Steps To Conserve Our Biodiversity -

  1. Stop Cutting Trees
  2. Reducing the wastage of water
  3. Stop Hunting Aniamls
  4. Minimising the use of the resources we get from the environment.
  5. Minimising Pollution.
